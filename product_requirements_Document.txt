### **2. Problem Statement**

*   **What specific problem are we trying to solve for whom?**
    *   Clearly articulate the pain point(s) or unmet need(s) of the target user or business. Use data or user quotes if available.
    *   *Example:* "Many individuals struggle to consistently drink enough water throughout the day (problem), leading to fatigue, reduced cognitive function, and other health issues (impact). Existing solutions are often generic and fail to adapt to individual needs and schedules (current limitations)."

### **3. Vision & Solution Overview**

*   **Vision:**
    *   What does success look like in the long term (1-3 years)? What ultimate impact will this product have?
    *   *Example:* "To empower individuals worldwide to achieve optimal hydration effortlessly, leading to improved health, energy, and overall well-being."
*   **Solution Overview:**
    *   Describe the proposed product or feature at a high level. What is it? How does it generally work? What's its core value proposition?
    *   *Example:* "We will develop a smart hydration tracking mobile app that integrates with wearables, offers customizable reminders based on user activity and climate, and provides insightful analytics on hydration patterns over time."

---

### **4. Goals & Objectives (SMART)**

*   **Business Goals:** (High-level, strategic)
    *   *Example:* "Increase customer retention by 15% within the next 12 months."
*   **Product Objectives:** (Specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound)
    *   *Example 1:* "Achieve a 4.5-star average rating on app stores within 3 months of launch."
    *   *Example 2:* "Onboard 50,000 active users (defined as logging water intake 3+ times a week) within 6 months of launch."
    *   *Example 3:* "Increase user engagement with personalized reminders by 20% compared to generic reminders, measured by reminder tap-through rate."

### **5. Target Audience**

*   **Who are the primary users?** (Demographics, psychographics, behaviors, needs)
    *   *Example:* "Health-conscious adults (25-45) who own smartphones/wearables, work desk jobs or are active, and are motivated to improve their well-being but often forget to drink water. They value convenience and personalized insights."
*   **Who are secondary users/stakeholders?**
    *   *Example:* "Fitness coaches or wellness professionals who might recommend the app to their clients."

### **6. Key Features (High-Level)**

*   List the core functionalities the product will offer. (This will be expanded into detailed user stories and functional requirements in a full PRD).
    *   **User Profile & Goal Setting:** Input personal data, set daily hydration goals.
    *   **Water Logging:** Easy entry for various fluid types and quantities.
    *   **Smart Reminders:** Customizable, context-aware reminders (time, location, activity).
    *   **Wearable Integration:** Sync with popular fitness trackers for activity-based adjustments.
    *   **Progress Tracking & Analytics:** Dashboards, charts, and historical data.
    *   **Gamification/Rewards (Optional):** Badges for streaks, challenges.

### **7. Success Metrics / KPIs**

*   How will we measure the success of this product, aligning with the objectives above?

    *   **Retention:** 7-day, 30-day retention rates.
    *   **Satisfaction:** App store ratings, NPS (Net Promoter Score).
    *   **Business Impact:** Revenue (if applicable), cost savings, market share.
